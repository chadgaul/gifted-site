How to Avoid Buying Duplicate Gifts

Every seasoned gift-giver has done it: bought a gift in October, forgotten it existed, and bought another in December - or unearthed a perfect, un-given present in February. The cause is the same, and so is the cure.

Why duplicates happen to organized people

Duplicate gifts aren't a memory problem - they're a bookkeeping problem. Early shopping (which is smart) creates a gap of weeks or months between buying a gift and giving it. Anything bought in that window exists only in your memory and a buried order-confirmation email. Add a good hiding spot and you've built a system for losing your own presents.

The failure has three flavors:

  • The double-buy: you forget October's purchase and buy December's replacement.
  • The lost gift: hidden so well it surfaces after the holiday.
  • The repeat: giving someone the same thing you gave them last year (or the year before).

The rule: log it when you buy it

The entire fix is one habit: the moment a gift is purchased, it gets logged - recipient, price, and where it's stashed. Not later, not "when I get home." A purchase that isn't logged immediately is a future duplicate.

How to do it in Gifted

  1. Mark it purchased on the spot. Each gift in Gifted has a status - flip it to purchased at checkout. The person's list now shows it plainly.
  2. Note the hiding spot. Every gift has a notes field: "top shelf, garage cabinet." February surprises solved.
  3. Check before you buy. The pre-purchase glance is the other half of the habit. Open the person in Gifted: their gifts and statuses are right there. Already covered? Put it down.
  4. Add a photo. For "is this the one I already bought?" moments in a store aisle, a photo answers instantly.
Tip: Gifted counts purchases per event - like "5/7 purchased" on your Christmas event - so you can see at a glance who's done and who still needs a gift, without opening a single closet.

Beating the year-over-year repeat

The subtler duplicate is the one across years: the same scarf, the same candle set, two Christmases running. Because Gifted's recurring events carry your history forward, last year's given gifts are a scroll away. Thirty seconds of review before you plan this year's gift eliminates the repeat entirely - and often sparks a better idea, since you can see what direction you've already gone.

A note on family double-buying

When two parents (or grandparents) shop independently, duplicates multiply. Even without shared accounts, a simple division works: agree on who owns which gifts, and let the person who tracks in Gifted be the source of truth for what's bought. One up-to-date list beats four good memories.

The payoff is bigger than avoiding waste

Never double-buying saves money and return trips, but the real win is confidence: shopping early stops feeling risky. When every purchase is logged with its hiding spot the moment it happens, you can buy a perfect gift in July and trust - not hope - that it will be under the tree in December.
